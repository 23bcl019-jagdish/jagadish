Project Overview: This project integrated Transportation Engineering, road safety, data observation, user behavior, and planning to improve movement at a selected road section.

Methodology

Observed traffic movement, identified conflict points, studied pedestrian safety, and suggested improvement measures.

Visual / Prototype

Traffic flow diagram, conflict point sketch, and safety improvement chart.

Visual Artefact: Traffic flow map + road safety improvement sketch

Global Awareness and Ethical Considerations

Reflection on sustainability, responsibility, and engineering ethics.

Global Perspective

Civil Engineering is closely connected with urbanization, climate change, resource use, road safety, housing, and public infrastructure. Sustainable design and responsible construction practices are important for long-term development.

Case Scenario: A construction project may appear successful if completed quickly, but poor drainage, weak quality control, or unsafe planning can create future risks for users and the environment.

Ethical Reflection

Engineering ethics include honest estimation, safe design, proper material selection, accurate measurements, quality control, and responsibility toward society.

Personal View: I believe a civil engineering project should be judged by safety, durability, usefulness, cost-effectiveness, and environmental responsibility.
